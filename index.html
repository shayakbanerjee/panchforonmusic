The Music

Panchforon is a group of six friends with years of association deeply rooted in music. Spread across the Greater New York area and Pittsburgh, we perform what is best described as Bengali diasporic music. We believe in a very inclusive definition of Bangla music and present a patchwork of the different genres that have, over time, shaped what we know as Bangla music of today. From long-forgotten baul songs to playback music from the black & white era, from the classic rock inspiration of Mohiner Ghoraguli to the jibonmukhi compositions of Anjan Dutta, we trace a line through the history of Bangla music. We even extrapolate that line with a few of our original compositions which range from toppa to rock.

 

The group has its roots in India in 2002, but formally came together in 2010. Panchforon has performed in Hartford (CT), Worcester (MA), Pittsburgh (PA), Buffalo, Rochester and Westchester County (NY). Our audience response has been excellent. People have loved our music. They have swayed to our romantic numbers. They have gotten off their seats and danced in the aisles to our foot-tapping songs. In post-performance conversations, they have also expressed their delight in our ability to engage everyone in the audience from the youngest to the oldest with the wide range of our repertoire.
